🥭 Mango Sticky Rice

A beloved Thai dessert with sweet glutinous rice, ripe mango slices, and rich coconut cream.

← Back to All Recipes
Prep Time Cook Time Servings Difficulty Category
15 min 25 min 4 Easy Dessert

About This Dish

Khao Niaow Ma Muang — Mango Sticky Rice — is Thailand's most beloved dessert and a symbol of the country's mango season (April–June). This deceptively simple dessert combines just three elements: perfectly steamed glutinous rice sweetened with coconut milk, ripe and fragrant mangoes, and a silky coconut cream sauce. The magic lies in the quality of the mango — it must be perfectly ripe, golden, and honey-sweet.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups glutinous (sticky) rice
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • 100ml coconut cream
  • 80g sugar (+ 1 tbsp for topping)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 ripe sweet mangoes
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Mint leaves for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rinse 1½ cups glutinous rice under cold water until clear. Soak in room temperature water for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight. This allows the rice to absorb water evenly for a tender, chewy texture.
  2. Drain the rice and steam in a bamboo steamer lined with cheesecloth over boiling water for 20–25 minutes. The rice is ready when translucent and sticky throughout with no chalky core.
  3. While the rice steams, warm 200ml coconut milk with 80g sugar and ¼ tsp salt in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves. Do not boil. Remove from heat.
  4. Transfer steamed rice to a large bowl and pour warm coconut sauce over it. Gently fold with a spatula. Cover with a clean towel and let rice absorb the coconut milk for 15–20 minutes, folding once halfway through.
  5. Prepare the topping: gently heat 100ml coconut cream with 1 tbsp sugar and a pinch of salt. Stir until combined and slightly thickened, about 2 minutes. Set aside.
  6. Peel and slice 2 ripe mangoes into even slices. Mound sticky rice on plates, fan mango slices alongside, drizzle with coconut cream sauce, and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds. Serve at room temperature.
