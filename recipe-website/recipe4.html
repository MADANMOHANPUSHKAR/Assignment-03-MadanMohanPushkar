🥗 Caesar Salad

Crisp romaine lettuce, crunchy croutons, shaved Parmesan, and creamy homemade Caesar dressing.

← Back to All Recipes
Prep Time Cook Time Servings Difficulty Category
10 min 5 min 2 Easy Vegetarian

About This Dish

The Caesar Salad is arguably the world's most iconic salad, created by Italian-American restaurateur Caesar Cardini in Tijuana, Mexico in 1924. What makes it special is the balance of textures and flavors — crisp, cold romaine lettuce against tangy, garlicky dressing, the crunch of golden croutons, and the salty bite of aged Parmesan.

Ingredients

  • 2 heads romaine lettuce
  • 3 slices day-old bread
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 5 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 60g Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Pinch of garlic powder
  • Italian dried herbs

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F). Cut bread into 1.5cm cubes. Toss with 2 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and Italian herbs. Bake for 10–12 minutes until golden and crunchy. Set aside to cool.
  2. Make the dressing: Whisk together mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and 3 tbsp grated Parmesan. Slowly drizzle in 3 tbsp olive oil while whisking until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Wash and thoroughly dry romaine lettuce. Tear leaves into large, bite-sized pieces — tearing creates more surface area for dressing to cling to.
  4. In a large serving bowl, add romaine leaves and drizzle about two-thirds of the dressing. Toss gently to coat every leaf evenly. Add more dressing to taste.
  5. Add cooled croutons and toss once more. Use a vegetable peeler to shave generous curls of Parmesan over the salad. Finish with black pepper and a squeeze of lemon. Serve immediately.
