🍚 Vegetable Biryani

Fragrant basmati rice layered with spiced mixed vegetables, saffron, and crispy fried onions.

← Back to All Recipes
Prep Time Cook Time Servings Difficulty Category
20 min 40 min 6 Medium Vegetarian

About This Dish

Vegetable Biryani is a celebration of aromatic spices and colorful vegetables, layered with perfectly cooked basmati rice. This Hyderabadi-inspired dum biryani is slow-cooked to perfection, allowing the fragrant saffron steam to infuse every grain. Whether served at a festive gathering or a cozy weeknight dinner, this dish never fails to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 medium potato, cubed
  • ½ cup cauliflower florets
  • 3 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ¼ cup yogurt
  • A pinch of saffron in 3 tbsp warm milk
  • Bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon
  • Fresh mint and cilantro leaves
  • Salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Wash 2 cups of basmati rice under running water until clear. Soak in cold water for 30 minutes, then drain. This ensures perfectly fluffy, separate grains.
  2. Boil a large pot of water with whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) and salt. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (about 5–6 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat 3 tablespoons of ghee in a heavy pot. Fry sliced onions until deep golden brown, about 10–12 minutes. Remove half for garnishing.
  4. To remaining onions, add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1 minute. Add mixed vegetables and cook 5 minutes. Add biryani masala, turmeric, and yogurt. Cook 3–4 minutes.
  5. Layer the partially cooked rice over the vegetables. Sprinkle saffron milk, fried onions, mint, cilantro, and dots of ghee. Cover tightly with foil and lid.
  6. Cook on high heat for 3 minutes, then reduce to lowest heat and cook (dum) for 25 minutes. Do not open the lid. Let rest 5 minutes before gently mixing. Serve with raita.
