🍝 Pasta Carbonara

Classic Roman pasta in a silky egg and cheese sauce with crispy pancetta and cracked black pepper.

← Back to All Recipes
Prep Time Cook Time Servings Difficulty Category
10 min 15 min 2 Medium Non-Veg

About This Dish

Pasta Carbonara is a beloved Roman classic that embodies the beauty of Italian cooking — a few quality ingredients combined with perfect technique. Authentic carbonara uses no cream whatsoever. The silky, velvety sauce is created entirely from eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and rendered fat from guanciale or pancetta. The trick is tossing hot pasta with the egg mixture off the heat.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
  • 150g guanciale or pancetta
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 whole egg
  • 60g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 30g Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
  • Freshly cracked black pepper (generous)
  • Salt for pasta water

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a rolling boil. Add 200g spaghetti and cook until al dente. Reserve a cup of starchy pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, cut 150g pancetta into small lardons. Place in a cold skillet and cook on medium heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden and crispy. Remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together 3 egg yolks, 1 whole egg, 60g Pecorino Romano, and 30g Parmigiano-Reggiano until you have a thick, creamy paste. Add generous black pepper.
  4. Add the drained hot pasta directly to the skillet with pancetta. Toss vigorously for 30 seconds so the pasta absorbs the rendered fat.
  5. Remove skillet completely from heat. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over hot pasta and toss quickly and continuously for about 60 seconds. The residual heat will cook the egg into a creamy sauce. Add pasta water one tablespoon at a time if too thick.
  6. Serve immediately on warm plates. Top with extra Pecorino Romano and a final crack of black pepper. Carbonara waits for no one — eat it the moment it's plated.
