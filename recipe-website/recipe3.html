🍫 Chocolate Lava Cake

A decadent individual chocolate cake with a warm, gooey molten center — the ultimate indulgent dessert.

← Back to All Recipes

15 min 14 min 4 Hard Dessert

About This Dish

The Chocolate Lava Cake — also known as molten chocolate cake — is the crown jewel of desserts. Invented by French-American chef Jean-Georges Vongerichten in 1987, this legendary dessert features a delicate outer shell that gives way to a river of warm, molten chocolate within. The secret lies in precise timing — a minute too long and you lose the lava; a minute too short and it collapses.

Ingredients

  • 120g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 120g unsalted butter
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 60g caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • Cocoa powder for dusting
  • Butter for greasing
  • Powdered sugar for garnish
  • Fresh raspberries to serve
  • Mint leaves for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Generously butter four 6-ounce ramekins, then dust with cocoa powder, tapping out the excess. Place ramekins on a baking sheet.
  2. Melt 120g dark chocolate with 120g unsalted butter in a double boiler. Stir gently until smooth and glossy. Remove from heat and cool for 2 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk 2 whole eggs and 2 egg yolks with 60g caster sugar until thick, pale, and doubled in volume — about 3–4 minutes with an electric whisk.
  4. Gently fold the melted chocolate into the egg mixture using a large spatula. Use slow, sweeping motions. Sift in 2 tablespoons of flour and fold until just combined.
  5. Divide batter evenly among prepared ramekins, filling each about three-quarters full. You can refrigerate up to 8 hours before baking.
  6. Bake for exactly 12–14 minutes. Edges should be firm but center will jiggle slightly. Let rest 1 minute, run a knife around edge, invert onto plate. Dust with powdered sugar and serve immediately with raspberries.
